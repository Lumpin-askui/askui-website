## Despite its effectiveness in specific domains, symbolic AI struggles with real-world complexity due to its dependence on explicit rules and handcrafted knowledge, making it brittle and inflexible. Identifying a cat in an image exemplifies this limitation— a purely symbolic AI system might rely on rigid rules such as "has fur," "has four legs," "has tail," which fall short in real-world scenarios where a cat may be partially obscured, viewed from different angles, or vary in lighting conditions.

## Neurosymbolic AI aims to bridge these gaps by merging deep learning's strengths with the reasoning abilities of symbolic AI. While deep learning excels at identifying patterns from vast data—key for tasks like image recognition and natural language processing—neurosymbolic AI combines this with the symbolic AI's logical reasoning. In the cat identification example, a neurosymbolic AI system can use a deep learning model trained on extensive image data to identify the cat regardless of variations in pose, lighting, or occlusion. This model serves as a perceptive engine, converting sensory data into symbolic forms that the symbolic AI component can manipulate.
