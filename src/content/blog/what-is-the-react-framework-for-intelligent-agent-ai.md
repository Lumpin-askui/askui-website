## Building Resilient Workflows

The ReACT framework operates through a structured process:

1.  **Reasoning:** The LLM thoroughly analyzes the given problem or task.
2.  **Acting:** Based on its reasoning, the LLM takes a specific action to gather more information or move closer to solving the problem.
3.  **Observation:** The LLM observes the outcome of its action, noting any changes in the environment or new information acquired.
4.  **Reflection:** The LLM reflects on the observation, updating its internal state and adjusting its strategy if necessary.
5.  **Iteration:** The cycle repeats, with the LLM reasoning, acting, observing, and reflecting until the problem is solved or a satisfactory solution is reached. [STAT: ReACT agents have demonstrated the ability to correct initial errors by adapting their strategy through iterative cycles.]
6.  **Final Output:** Once the iterative process converges, the LLM provides the final answer or executes the final action.

## FAQ

### What makes ReACT different from traditional AI models?

ReACT distinguishes itself by integrating reasoning and action in a continuous loop. Traditional AI models often focus on one or the other in isolation, limiting their ability to adapt and improve during problem-solving. ReACT, on the other hand, allows for iterative refinement through observation and reflection.

### How does ReACT handle errors during the problem-solving process?

ReACT is designed to identify and correct errors through its iterative process. After each action, the AI observes the outcome and reflects on whether the action brought it closer to the solution. If an error is detected, the AI adjusts its strategy and takes a different action in the next iteration.
